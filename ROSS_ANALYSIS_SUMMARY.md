## Top 3 Priority Study Areas (50% of questions to review)

### 1. Heat & Thermal Energy - 8 questions (20%)

**Key Concepts to Master:**
- Heat flows from HOT → COLD (not cold to hot)
- Understanding energy absorption vs. release
- Objects absorb thermal energy from warmer surroundings
- Temperature difference affects heat transfer rate
- Evaporative cooling (evaporation absorbs energy)

**Study Priority:** HIGH - Focusing here will make the biggest impact

**Quick Reference:**
- → Always: Heat flows from warmer object to cooler object
- → Greater temp difference = faster heat transfer
- → Evaporation removes thermal energy (cooling effect)

---

### 2. Newton's Laws / Force & Motion - 7 questions (17.5%)

**Key Concepts to Master:**
- F=ma (Force = mass × acceleration)
- Understanding: more mass = LESS acceleration with same force
- Newton's 3rd Law (action-reaction pairs)
- In collisions, both objects exert equal force on each other

**Study Priority:** HIGH - Foundational physics concept

**Quick Reference:**
- → F = ma: Same force, double mass = HALF acceleration
- → Newton's 3rd: Forces ALWAYS come in equal & opposite pairs
- → In collisions: Both objects exert equal force on each other

---

### 3. Electromagnetic Spectrum & Waves - 5 questions (12.5%)

**Key Concepts to Master:**
- EM spectrum order from longest to shortest wavelength
- Radio waves have longest wavelength, Gamma rays have shortest
- Amplitude-energy relationship: higher amplitude = more energy

**Study Priority:** HIGH - Straightforward memorization that brings quick wins

**Quick Reference:**
- → EM Spectrum: Radio > Microwave > Infrared > Visible > UV > X-ray > Gamma
- → RADIO = Longest wavelength, GAMMA = Shortest wavelength
- → Higher amplitude = MORE energy

### 1. ACTIVE RECALL (most effective)
- Don't just read notes → Cover and try to explain concept out loud

- Show all work, even for "easy" calculations

- Identify remaining areas for improvement
