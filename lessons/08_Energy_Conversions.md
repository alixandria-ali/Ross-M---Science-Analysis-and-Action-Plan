## Key Concepts to Master

### Two Main Types of Mechanical Energy

**1. Potential Energy (PE)** - Stored energy based on position
- Energy of position or height
- The HIGHER an object, the MORE potential energy
- Formula: PE = mass × gravity × height (PE = mgh)

**2. Kinetic Energy (KE)** - Energy of motion
- Energy of movement
- The FASTER an object moves, the MORE kinetic energy
- Formula: KE = ½ × mass × velocity² (KE = ½mv²)

---

## The Critical Relationship: Height and Energy

### The Fundamental Rule:

**HIGHER starting position → MORE potential energy → MORE kinetic energy when it falls**

Think of a water slide:
- Start at TOP = lots of PE, zero KE (not moving yet)
- As you slide down = PE converts to KE (speeding up)
- At BOTTOM = minimal PE, maximum KE (moving fastest)

---

## Energy Conversion Examples

### Roller Coaster 🎢
- **Top of first hill:** Maximum PE, minimum KE (high up, moving slowly)
- **Falling down:** PE → KE (height decreases, speed increases)
- **Bottom of hill:** Minimum PE, maximum KE (low point, moving fastest)
- **Going up next hill:** KE → PE (speed decreases, height increases)

### Pendulum Swing
- **Highest points:** Maximum PE, zero KE (not moving at ends)
- **Lowest point:** Minimum PE, maximum KE (moving fastest at bottom)

### Dropped Object
- **Before drop:** All PE (high up, not moving)
- **During fall:** PE → KE (losing height, gaining speed)
- **Just before hitting ground:** Maximum KE (moving fastest)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid ⚠️

### ❌ WRONG: "Lower starting height = more kinetic energy at impact"
### ✅ CORRECT: "HIGHER starting height = more kinetic energy at impact"

### ❌ WRONG: "At the top of a roller coaster, kinetic energy is maximum"
### ✅ CORRECT: "At the top, POTENTIAL energy is maximum; kinetic is minimum"

### ❌ WRONG: "Energy is created when an object falls"
### ✅ CORRECT: "Energy is CONVERTED from PE to KE (total energy stays the same)"

---

## The Law of Conservation of Energy

**Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.**

**Total Energy = PE + KE = constant** (ignoring friction/air resistance)

When PE decreases → KE increases by the same amount
When KE decreases → PE increases by the same amount

A. Position 1 (highest)
B. Position 2
C. Position 3
D. Position 4 (lowest)

**Answer: A**

- Higher position = MORE potential energy

- More PE at start = More KE at impact

Think of it this way: Drop a ball from 10 feet vs 2 feet - which hits harder? The one dropped from 10 feet! That's because it had more PE to convert to KE.

Formula: PE = mgh (more height = more PE → more KE when falling)

A. Potential → kinetic
B. Kinetic → potential
C. Thermal → chemical
D. Electrical → kinetic

<details>

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** As the roller coaster goes DOWN a hill, it loses height (PE decreases) and gains speed (KE increases). The transformation is PE → KE. Going DOWN = losing PE, gaining KE. Going UP = losing KE, gaining PE.

### Question 4
A pendulum swings back and forth. At which point does it have the MOST kinetic energy?

A. At the highest point on the left
B. At the lowest point (center)
C. At the highest point on the right
D. Kinetic energy is constant throughout

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** At the LOWEST point, the pendulum is moving FASTEST, so it has maximum kinetic energy. At the HIGHEST points (both sides), the pendulum momentarily stops before reversing direction, so KE = 0 and PE is maximum.

Energy pattern:
- Top: Max PE, zero KE
- Middle (bottom): Min PE, max KE
- Other top: Max PE, zero KE

</details>

### Question 5
Two identical balls are dropped from different heights. Ball A drops from 10 m, Ball B drops from 5 m. Which has more kinetic energy just before hitting the ground?

A. Ball A
B. Ball B
C. Same kinetic energy
D. Cannot determine

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Ball A starts with MORE potential energy (higher height). All of that PE converts to KE as it falls. More PE at start → more KE at impact. Ball A started at 10 m (double the height), so it has twice the PE, which converts to twice the KE at impact.

PE = mgh
Ball A: PE = mg(10) = 10mg
Ball B: PE = mg(5) = 5mg

Ball A has 2× the PE, so 2× the KE at impact!

</details>

<details>

The ball's KE does work on the pins. Without kinetic energy (if the ball were just sitting still), it couldn't knock down any pins.

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The roller coaster moves fastest at the LOWEST point. At this point:

### Question 8
A skier at the top of a mountain has 10,000 J of potential energy. Ignoring friction, how much kinetic energy will the skier have at the bottom?

A. 0 J
B. 5,000 J
C. 10,000 J
D. 20,000 J

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Conservation of energy! The total energy stays constant (ignoring friction). At the top: PE = 10,000 J, KE = 0 J. At the bottom: PE = 0 J, KE = ? The 10,000 J of PE must convert to 10,000 J of KE. Total energy remains 10,000 J throughout!

Top: 10,000 PE + 0 KE = 10,000 J total
Bottom: 0 PE + 10,000 KE = 10,000 J total

</details>

### Question 9
Two marbles roll at the same speed. Marble X has mass 20 g, Marble Y has mass 40 g. Which has more kinetic energy?

A. Marble X
B. Marble Y
C. Same kinetic energy
D. Cannot determine

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** KE = ½mv². Since speed (v) is the same, the marble with MORE MASS has more KE. Marble Y has twice the mass, so it has twice the kinetic energy. Both speed AND mass affect kinetic energy.

Marble X: KE = ½(20)v² = 10v²
Marble Y: KE = ½(40)v² = 20v²

Marble Y has 2× the KE!

</details>

### Question 10
A ball is thrown straight up. At what point is its kinetic energy at maximum?

A. When it leaves your hand
B. At the top of its path
C. Halfway up
D. Just before landing back in your hand

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: A (and D - both are correct!)

**Explanation:** KE is maximum when the ball is moving FASTEST. This occurs:
- When it LEAVES your hand (moving fastest upward)
- When it RETURNS to your hand (moving fastest downward)

At the TOP, the ball momentarily stops (v = 0), so KE = 0 and PE is maximum. As it rises, KE → PE. As it falls, PE → KE.

</details>

## Quick Reference Card 📋

**Potential Energy (PE):**
- Energy of HEIGHT/POSITION
- PE = mgh (mass × gravity × height)
- **Higher = MORE PE**

**Kinetic Energy (KE):**
- Energy of MOTION
- KE = ½mv² (½ × mass × speed²)
- **Faster = MORE KE**

**Energy Conversions:**
- Going UP: KE → PE (slowing down, gaining height)
- Going DOWN: PE → KE (gaining speed, losing height)

**Conservation of Energy:**
- Total energy stays constant (PE + KE = constant)
- Energy transforms, never created or destroyed

**Key Rule:**
**HIGHEST starting point → MOST kinetic energy at bottom**

---

## Memory Trick 🧠

**"High and Slow → Low and Fast"**
- High position = High PE, Low KE (slow)
- Low position = Low PE, High KE (fast)

Think of a ski jump:
- **Top of ramp:** High, slow (lots of PE)
- **Bottom/takeoff:** Low, FAST (lots of KE)
