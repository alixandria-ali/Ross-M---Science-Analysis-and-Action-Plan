## Key Concepts to Master

### The Speed Formula

**Speed = Distance ÷ Time**

or written as: **v = d/t**

Where:
- **v** = velocity/speed
- **d** = distance traveled
- **t** = time taken

---

## Rearranging the Formula

From **v = d/t**, you can solve for any variable:

| To find... | Use this formula | Example |
|------------|------------------|---------|
| **Speed** | v = d/t | d = 100 m, t = 10 s → v = 100/10 = 10 m/s |
| **Distance** | d = v×t | v = 5 m/s, t = 20 s → d = 5×20 = 100 m |
| **Time** | t = d/v | d = 60 km, v = 30 km/h → t = 60/30 = 2 h |

---

## Understanding Average Speed

**Average Speed** = Total Distance ÷ Total Time

**Important:** Average speed is NOT the same as average of speeds!

**Example:**
- Drive 60 km at 30 km/h (takes 2 hours)
- Drive 40 km at 40 km/h (takes 1 hour)
- Average speed = (60+40)/(2+1) = 100/3 = 33.3 km/h
- NOT (30+40)/2 = 35 km/h ❌

---

## Comparing Speeds from Data Tables

When given a table with distance and time data:

**Step 1:** Calculate speed for each object using v = d/t
**Step 2:** Compare the calculated speeds
**Step 3:** Choose the object with the HIGHEST speed value

### Example Table:

| Object | Distance | Time | Speed (calculate!) |
|--------|----------|------|-------------------|
| Object 1 | 100 m | 10 s | 100/10 = 10 m/s |
| Object 2 | 150 m | 20 s | 150/20 = 7.5 m/s |

**Answer:** Object 1 has greater speed (10 m/s > 7.5 m/s)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid ⚠️

### ❌ WRONG: "Greater distance always means greater speed"
### ✅ CORRECT: "Speed depends on BOTH distance AND time (v = d/t)"

### ❌ WRONG: "Average speed = average of the speeds"
### ✅ CORRECT: "Average speed = total distance ÷ total time"

### ❌ WRONG: "Longer time means greater speed"
### ✅ CORRECT: "Longer time with same distance means SLOWER speed"

---

## Units of Speed

Common speed units (distance/time):
- **m/s** (meters per second) - science standard
- **km/h** (kilometers per hour) - cars
- **mi/h or mph** (miles per hour) - US cars
- **cm/s** (centimeters per second) - small objects

**Always check units!** Make sure distance and time units match.

## Practice Questions

### Question 1
A car travels 150 km in 3 hours. What is its average speed?

A. 50 km/h
B. 150 km/h
C. 450 km/h
D. 3 km/h

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
Speed = 150 km ÷ 3 hours = 50 km/h

The car travels an average of 50 kilometers every hour.

</details>

### Question 2
Two runners race 100 meters. Runner A finishes in 10 seconds. Runner B finishes in 12 seconds. Who has the greater average speed?

A. Runner A
B. Runner B
C. Same speed
D. Cannot determine

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Both run the same distance (100 m), but Runner A takes LESS time.
- Runner A: v = 100/10 = 10 m/s
- Runner B: v = 100/12 = 8.33 m/s

Runner A is faster (10 m/s > 8.33 m/s).

**Remember:** Same distance, less time = greater speed!

</details>

### Question 3
A distance/time table shows:

Which object has greater average speed?

A. Object 1
B. Object 2
C. Same speed
D. Cannot determine

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
Calculate speed for both:
- Object 1: v = 80/20 = 4 m/s
- Object 2: v = 60/10 = 6 m/s

Object 2 is faster! Even though it traveled less distance, it took much less time, resulting in a higher speed.

**Key lesson:** You MUST calculate; you can't just look at distance or time alone!

### Question 7
Which statement is true about speed?

A. Higher speed means more distance traveled in the same time
B. Speed is independent of distance
C. Faster objects take more time to travel the same distance
D. Speed only depends on distance

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Speed is the rate of covering distance over time. Higher speed means you cover MORE distance in the SAME amount of time. Or equivalently, you cover the same distance in LESS time.

Examples:
- 10 m/s for 5 seconds = 50 m
- 20 m/s for 5 seconds = 100 m (twice as fast = twice the distance)

</details>

### Question 8
A table shows:
- Car A: travels 240 km in 4 hours
- Car B: travels 200 km in 4 hours

Which car travels faster?

A. Car A
B. Car B
C. Same speed
D. Cannot determine

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Same time (4 hours), but different distances:
- Car A: v = 240/4 = 60 km/h
- Car B: v = 200/4 = 50 km/h

Car A is faster. With the same time, whoever goes farther is faster!

**Shortcut:** When time is the same, just compare distances!

### Question 9
Sound travels 330 m in 1 second. Light travels 300,000,000 m in 1 second. How many times faster is light than sound?

A. About 100 times
B. About 1,000 times
C. About 10,000 times
D. About 900,000 times

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer</summary>

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:**
Speed of light ÷ Speed of sound = 300,000,000 / 330 ≈ 909,000

Light is about 900,000 times faster than sound! This is why you see lightning before you hear thunder - light reaches you almost instantly, but sound takes time to travel.

## Quick Reference Card 📋

**The Triangle Trick:**
```
     d
   -----
   |   |
   v × t
```

Cover what you're looking for:
- Cover **d** → see v × t → Distance = Speed × Time
- Cover **v** → see d/t → Speed = Distance ÷ Time
- Cover **t** → see d/v → Time = Distance ÷ Speed

**Key Formulas:**
- **v = d/t** (speed = distance ÷ time)
- **d = v×t** (distance = speed × time)
- **t = d/v** (time = distance ÷ speed)

**Comparing Speeds:**
1. Always CALCULATE (don't just guess)
2. Use v = d/t for each object
3. Compare the numbers

**Units to Know:**
- m/s (meters per second)
- km/h (kilometers per hour)
- Always make sure units match!

## Common Sense Checks ✓

After calculating, ask yourself:
- **Does the number make sense?**
  - Running speed: 2-10 m/s ✓
  - Car speed: 50-120 km/h ✓
  - Sound speed: ~330 m/s ✓
  - Light speed: 300,000 km/s ✓

- **Are my units correct?**
  - Distance in meters → time in seconds → speed in m/s
  - Distance in km → time in hours → speed in km/h

---

## Data Interpretation Tips

When looking at tables:
1. **Identify** what you're comparing
2. **Calculate** the value (don't estimate)
3. **Compare** the calculated values
4. **Choose** based on the question (greatest? least? same?)

**Never assume based on appearance - always calculate!**
